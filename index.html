    Los Angeles has one million residents.

    Los Angeles City Hall opens.
  • 1929
    UCLA moves to its current Westwood location.
  • 1932
    Los Angeles hosts the Olympic Games for the first time.

    The Arroyo Seco Parkway Downtown and Pasadena becomes the nation's first freeway.

    The Cleveland Rams football team moves to Los Angeles.
  • 1955
    Disneyland opens in Anaheim, CA.
  • 1957
    Building height restrictions lifted by voter referendum.
  • 1958
    The Los Angeles Dodgers become the West's first major league baseball team.
  • 1960
    The Minneapolis Lakers becomes LA's first basketball team.
  • 1962
    The last of the Red Car trolleys ceases operation.
  • 1965
    Restrictions are lifted on immigration from East Asia, leading to the establishment of Koreatown.
  • 1965
    Watts riots take place.
  • 1973
    Tom Bradley becomes mayor of Los Angeles and the second black mayor of a major United States city.
  • 1911
    Los Angeles begins regulating air pollution.
  • 1984
    Los Angeles' population surpasses 3 million, overtakes Chicago as the nation's second largest city.
  • 1984
    Los Angeles becomes the only American city to host the summer Olympic games twice.
  • 1990
    US Bank Tower opens.

    Metro Blue Line opens as the city's first modern rapid transit line.
  • 1992
    Rodney King riots take place.
  • 1994
    O.J. Simpson trial takes place.
  • 2005
    Antonino Villaraigosa becomes the city's first Hispanic mayor since 1872.
  • 2006
    Population of L.A. County surpasses 10 million.
  • 2013
    Eric Garcetti becomes the city's first Jewish mayor.
  • 2017
    Wilshire Grand Tower completed, surpassing the U.S. Bank Tower as the tallest building west of the Mississippi River.
